Hannigan is a singer-songwriter inspired by the changing world around him. Drawing lyrics from his surroundings he is able to deliver a unique style of story telling that he has mastered over a number of years. Taking inspiration from artists such as Paolo Nutini, Passenger and Hozier to name a few, he has created a sound that can only be defined as his own.

Early in 2017, upon moving to Cornwall The Waves was given the support of BBC Introducing Cornwall.

After moving to Falmouth, Cornwall and leaving his band in Kent, he found himself drifting further away from the electric style of recording he had previously aspired to in his 2017 work, and evolved once again into a solo acoustic artist.

This regained love for acoustic folk music lead him to his most recent recorded work, a live acoustic EP named Live at 71. Recorded and mastered by Chris Delaney, Live at 71 was a project to show Hannigan's ever-evolving style of songwriting and an accurate representation of his live performance in 2018.
